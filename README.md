# Opportunities and Challenges of Scaling Agile Software Development

This repository contains my admission essay for the **M.Sc. in Information Systems** program at the Technical University of Munich (TUM).

## Overview

- **Title:** Opportunities and Challenges of Scaling Agile Software Development Methods in Large Established Organizations  
- **Author:** Cheng Chih-Chuan  
- **Word Count:** 1067  
- **Submission Date:** November 27, 2025  

This essay explores the limitations of traditional software development models and analyzes how agile methodologies can be adapted and scaled in large organizations.

---

## Purpose

The purpose of this essay is to:

- Examine the transition from traditional **Waterfall model** to **Agile methodologies**
- Analyze the **challenges** of scaling agile in large organizations
- Identify key **opportunities and benefits** of large-scale agile adoption
- Discuss how organizations can **adapt agile frameworks effectively**

---

## Key Topics Covered

### 1. Introduction & Theoretical Background
- Waterfall model limitations (rigidity, assumption of stable requirements)
- Emergence of Agile (Agile Manifesto, 2001)
- Popular frameworks:
  - Scrum
  - Extreme Programming (XP)
  - SAFe (Scaled Agile Framework)

### 2. Challenges of Scaling Agile
- Cross-team coordination issues
  - Misalignment between agile and non-agile departments (e.g., marketing)
- Organizational structure complexity
  - Holacracy structure ( High Flexibility v.s. Role Ambiguity )
- Cultural resistance to the agile mindset
  - Nokia’s agile transformation challenges

### 3. Opportunities
- Improved transparency across teams and stakeholders
- Better dependency management between teams
- Reduced development cost through continuous feedback

### 4. Key Insight
There is **no one-size-fits-all agile framework**. Thus, this perspective highlights a key research gap: identifying which organizational structures best fit different large-scale contexts to enable more effective agile adoption.
